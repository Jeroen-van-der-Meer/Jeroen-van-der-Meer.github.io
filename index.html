Jeroen van der Meer

I am a mathematician by training, currently employed at the University of Copen­hagen, with a passion for anything computer science and physics.

Interests

I am mainly interested in the application of homotopy theory to other branches of mathematics. Historically, homotopy theory was a branch of algebraic topology in which spaces were studied 'up to continuous deformation'; in modern days, however, homotopy theory has grown out of its initial contraints, having proved itself to be spectacularly useful in many other areas of mathematics, such as arithmetic geometry, K-theory, and theoretical physics.

My research mainly concerned itself with the application of homotopy theory to group theory. Specifically, I proved that certain invariants arising from the modular representation theory of finite groups can be reinterpreted in higher-categorical language, after which I was able to invoke powerful homotopical machinery to explicitly compute these invariants.

More recently, I've come to be more interested in the formal aspects of homotopy theory. Homotopy type theory is a foundation of mathematics in which $\infty$-groupoids, rather than sets, are taken to be the basic objects, allowing for a synthetic approach to higher-coherent mathematics. Being a flavour of type theory, it is naturally amenable to computer formalisation, thus allowing, among many things, for formal computations of the homotopy groups of spheres.

Outside of mathematics, I enjoy working with computers. Although my main experience comes from writing and studying mathematical models, I also like to think about other topics, such as computer architecture and cryptography.

Papers

  • Jeroen van der Meer. A homotopical characterisation of the Dade group.

    We give a homotopical interpretation of the Dade group of a finite $p$-group $G$. Specifically, we show that it can be viewed as the Picard group of a certain $\infty$-category, which may be regarded as the geometric fixed points of the category of $G$-module spectra.

    Forthcoming.

  • Jeroen van der Meer, Richard Wong. Endotrivial modules for cyclic $p$-groups and generalized quaternion groups via Galois descent.

    In this paper, we investigate the group of endotrivial modules for certain $p$-groups. Such groups were already been computed by Carlson–Thévenaz using the theory of support varieties; however, we provide novel homotopical proofs of their results for cyclic $p$-groups, the quaternion group of order 8, and for generalized quaternion groups using Galois descent and Picard spectral sequences, building on results of Mathew and Stojanoska. Our computations provide conceptual insights into the classical work of Carlson–Thévenaz.

    Forthcoming.

  • Jeroen van der Meer. A stack-theoretic perspective on $\mathrm{KU}$-local stable homotopy theory.

    We study the structure of the $\mathrm{KU}$-local stable homotopy category using the language of algebraic stacks. Specifically, we show that the $\mathrm{KU}$-local stable homotopy category can be described in terms of coherent sheaves on the moduli stack of formal groups.
